Ferrari F40

Production started for the first time in 1987. The body of the vehicle, which is produced purely for performance, is made of carbon fiber and no luxuries or accessories are used to cause weight.One of the two twin-turbo models produced by Ferrari has been the Ferrari F40. The F40 is the last Ferrari car that founder Enzo Ferrari played a role in building. at the time of the vehicle's first introduction in 1987, he expressed his thoughts about the F40 with the words "I told my engineers to produce the best sports car in the world, and now I'm standing next to him". it produces 478 horsepower and 575 nm torque from the 2.9 liter engine. 0-100 = 4.1 seconds, the top speed is 313 km/h.

Ferrari F355

The Ferrari F355 is a sports car produced by the Italian automobile manufacturer Ferrari between Dec 1994 and 1999. The 3.5-liter V8 gasoline engine with a power of 380 hp provided the power of the F355. It is an improved model of the Ferrari 348 and was replaced by the Ferrari 360 in 1999. with 11,273 units produced, the model became one of Ferrari's most produced series with this number. At first, the model came out with two options: the Berlinetta as a coupe, and the targa model came out as a GTS. The Spider version was released in 1995. in 1998, the Ferrari 355 F1 version with a Formula 1-style transmission was produced. 0–100 km/s 4.7 s, the top speed is 295 km/h.

Ferrari Testarossa

The Testarossa has a naturally aspirated 4.9 L (4,943 cc) longitudinally-mounted. Maximum power of 287 kW (390 PS; 385 hp) at 6,300 rpm and maximum torque of 490 N⋅m (361 lb⋅ft) at 4,500 rpm. The Testarossa can accelerate from 0–100 km/h (62 mph) in 5.8 seconds. The top speed of the Testarossa is estimated at 290 km/h.The Pininfarina-designed car was originally produced from 1984 until 1991, with two model revisions following the end of Testarossa production called the 512 TR and F512 M, which were produced from 1992 until 1996. Including revised variations, almost 10,000 cars in total were produced, making it at the time one of the most mass-produced Ferrari models.

Ferrari 250 GTO

The Ferrari 250 GTO is a grand tourer produced by Ferrari from 1962 to 1964 for homologation into the FIA's Group 3 Grand Touring Car category. Just 36 of the 250 GTOs were manufactured between 1962 and 1964. An all-alloy design utilizing a dry sump and six 38DCN Weber carburetors, it produced approximately 300 PS (296 bhp; 221 kW) at 7500 rpm and 294 N⋅m; 217 lbf⋅ft (30 kg⋅m) at 5500 rpm of torque. While the GTO is now arguably the most valuable collector car in the world, it was merely a no-frills used race car in the late 1960s and very early 1970s. The current record for world's most expensive Ferrari was set in June 2018 when a 1963 250 GTO (chassis 4153GT) was sold to David MacNeil in a private sale for $70 million.
